# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Casual groups — roommates, trip companions, friend circles — who need to track and settle shared expenses together without anyone creating an account. Anyone with the group's link can add expenses or people; there's no primary "owner" role. The core context is often mid-trip or mid-month, on a phone, wanting to log something quickly and move on, not sit and configure a tool.

## Product Purpose

Splitto combines the parts of Splitwise, Tricount, and Spliit that actually matter for a group tracking shared money: Splitwise's item-level splitting and spending analytics, Tricount's zero-friction account-less sharing via a single link, and Spliit's open, ad-free, non-paywalled ethos. Success looks like a group finishing a trip or a shared living arrangement with an accurate, trusted record of who owes whom, arrived at with the least possible friction along the way.

## Positioning

A free, open, privacy-first way for a group to track shared money together — no account required for anyone, ever.

## Brand Personality

Calm and minimal — quiet, unhurried, generous whitespace, neutral color use. The interface should feel closer to a well-kept shared notebook than a finance dashboard: legible, unshowy, and trustworthy without needing to prove it with polish.

## Anti-references

Splitwise's busier, ad-supported feel — dense toolbars, upsell banners, and general visual noise. Splitto should never feel like it's selling the user something or competing for their attention against the actual task of settling up.

## Design Principles

Zero friction beats feature completeness — a forced signup or an extra required field loses to getting the expense logged. Show the real computed numbers, not just categories or checkboxes — a live dollar share is more trustworthy than an abstract split indicator. Recoverable beats restrictive — soft-delete, undo, and archive (never a hard, scary destructive action) so nobody's afraid to make a change. Remember preferences per-device, not per-account — local defaults (last payer, last visit) deliver the convenience of a login without requiring one. Flag, don't block — possible problems (a likely duplicate expense, a fallback exchange rate) are surfaced for a human to judge, never silently auto-corrected or used to stop someone from saving.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target or known specific user needs at this time. Default to sensible practice — sufficient color contrast, readable text sizes, no motion-dependent affordances — without a stricter mandated baseline.
